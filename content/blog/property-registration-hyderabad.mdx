Property registration is not a formality — it is the legal act that transfers ownership. An unregistered sale deed gives you no title to the property in the eyes of the law. In Hyderabad, which has one of India's more active real estate markets, understanding the registration process is essential for any buyer. This guide takes you through the entire process, from title verification to collecting your registered document from the Sub-Registrar's Office.

## Why Registration is Mandatory

Under the **Registration Act 1908**, any document that creates, assigns, limits, extinguishes, or declares rights in immovable property worth Rs. 100 or more must be compulsorily registered. A sale deed for any property — land, flat, commercial space — falls squarely within this requirement.

An unregistered sale deed cannot be used as evidence of title in any court of law. It also cannot be mutated in revenue records, meaning the property stays in the seller's name on government records — a situation that creates serious complications, particularly in inheritance, further sales, or loan applications.

## Documents That Must Be Registered

The most common documents registered at Sub-Registrar Offices (SROs) in Hyderabad include:

- Sale deeds (purchase of land, flats, or commercial property)
- Gift deeds
- Partition deeds
- Settlement deeds
- Mortgage deeds
- Exchange deeds
- Power of Attorney (when relating to immovable property)
- Long-term lease agreements (exceeding 11 months)

## Step 1 — Title Verification

Before paying stamp duty or approaching the SRO, verify the property's title thoroughly. This is a step many buyers skip in their eagerness, and it creates problems later.

Title verification involves:

- **Examining the chain of title** going back at least 30 years — each conveyance (sale, gift, partition, inheritance) should be traceable and properly documented
- **Obtaining an Encumbrance Certificate (EC)** from the SRO for the relevant period — the EC shows all registered transactions on the property and reveals mortgages, loans, or prior sales
- **Verifying ownership in revenue records (Pahani/Record of Rights)** through the Dharani portal for agricultural lands, or property tax records through GHMC for urban properties
- **Checking for pending litigation** through a search at the Civil Courts with jurisdiction
- **Verifying building plan approval** from GHMC, HMDA, or the relevant authority if you are buying a constructed property

A lawyer's title opinion, based on this investigation, is worth the cost — especially for high-value properties.

## Step 2 — Stamp Duty Payment

Stamp duty is a state government tax on the instrument of transfer and must be paid before registration. In Telangana, the applicable rates are:

| Transaction Type | Stamp Duty | Registration Fee | Transfer Duty |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sale of property (other than agricultural land) | 4% of market value | 0.5% (capped at Rs. 20,000 for residential) | 1.5% |
| Gift (to blood relatives) | 2% | 0.5% | 0.5% |
| Gift (to others) | 4% + surcharge | 0.5% | 1.5% |
| Partition deed | Varies by share | 0.5% | — |

**Total effective cost for a typical sale deed in Telangana: approximately 6% of the property's market value** (4% stamp duty + 0.5% registration fee + 1.5% transfer duty).

### Market Value vs. Consideration Value

Stamp duty is calculated on the **higher of** the actual sale consideration or the government's determined market value (the "guideline value" or "basic value"). The guideline values for each locality in Hyderabad are published on the IGRS Telangana portal (igrs.telangana.gov.in) and are updated periodically.

If you are buying a property for Rs. 80 lakhs but the government guideline value for that property is Rs. 90 lakhs, stamp duty is calculated on Rs. 90 lakhs — not on the actual sale price. This is a common source of surprise for first-time buyers.

Stamp duty is paid by purchasing **non-judicial stamp paper** of the required value from authorised vendors, or through the **Stamps and Registration Integrated Payment System** (SARATHI), which allows online payment and issuance of a franking receipt. The SARATHI system is the preferred method for large transactions in Hyderabad.

## Step 3 — Drafting the Sale Deed

The sale deed is the legal document that evidences the transfer of property. It must be carefully drafted to include:

- Full details of the seller and buyer (name, father's name, address, Aadhaar, PAN)
- Complete property description including Survey Number, plot dimensions, boundaries (all four sides), and municipal/revenue details
- Sale consideration and mode of payment
- Confirmation of clear title and freedom from encumbrances
- Possession clause
- Indemnity clause (seller indemnifying the buyer against title defects)
- Signatures of both parties and two witnesses (with their details)

The deed should be drafted by a qualified lawyer. Errors in the property description or party details can render the document legally defective and create significant problems later.

## Step 4 — Slot Booking on IGRS Portal

Hyderabad Sub-Registrar Offices now operate on an appointment system. Slot booking is done online through the **IGRS Telangana portal (igrs.telangana.gov.in)**:

1. Register on the IGRS portal with your mobile number
2. Navigate to "Slot Booking for Document Registration"
3. Select the appropriate SRO based on the property's location (jurisdiction follows the location of the property, not the parties' addresses)
4. Choose a date and time slot
5. Confirm the booking — you will receive an SMS with the appointment reference

Walk-in registrations are not available at most Hyderabad SROs. Missing your slot means rebooking, which can delay matters by days.

## Step 5 — The SRO Visit

On the appointment day, all parties must be present at the SRO. Carry:

**Documents to bring:**

- Original sale deed (on stamp paper or with SARATHI franking receipt) — two copies
- Encumbrance Certificate (original + copy)
- Property tax receipt for the current year
- Previous title documents (parent documents)
- No-Objection Certificate (NOC) from the Housing Society or Apartment Association if applicable
- Aadhaar card (mandatory for biometric verification) — seller, buyer, and both witnesses
- PAN card of seller and buyer
- Passport-size photographs
- Proof of stamp duty payment
- Slot booking confirmation

## Step 6 — Biometric Verification and Document Submission

At the SRO, the process unfolds as follows:

1. **Document scrutiny:** The SRO staff examines the documents for completeness, verifies stamp duty payment, and checks that the correct SRO has jurisdiction
2. **Biometric capture:** Fingerprints and photographs of all parties (seller, buyer, witnesses) are captured — this is mandatory and prevents impersonation
3. **Admission:** The seller formally "admits" the document — confirms their identity, acknowledgement of the deed, and consent to registration
4. **Document scanning:** The executed deed is scanned and uploaded to the IGRS system
5. **Endorsement:** The Sub-Registrar endorses the document, recording the registration details (book number, volume, document number, date)
6. **Collection:** The original registered deed is returned, typically the same day for most Hyderabad SROs, or the next working day at most

The registered document bears the SRO's official seal and endorsement — this is what establishes legal title.

## TDS on Property Purchases Above Rs. 50 Lakhs

If the sale consideration exceeds Rs. 50 lakhs, the buyer is required to deduct **Tax Deducted at Source (TDS)** at 1% of the sale consideration under Section 194-IA of the Income Tax Act and deposit it with the government before registration.

The process:
1. The buyer deducts 1% from the payment to the seller
2. The buyer files **Form 26QB** on the Income Tax portal and pays the TDS
3. The buyer downloads Form 16B (TDS certificate) and gives it to the seller

Failure to deduct and deposit TDS attracts interest, penalties, and prosecution. This is a step frequently overlooked in the excitement of closing a transaction — do not skip it.

## Mutation After Registration

Registration at the SRO transfers legal title. But to update government records — revenue records, property tax records, GHMC records — you must complete **mutation (name transfer).**

For properties within GHMC limits, apply for mutation at the concerned GHMC Zonal Office or online through the GHMC portal, attaching the registered sale deed and other documents. Property tax will then be assessed in the buyer's name.

For properties in HMDA or panchayat areas, the mutation process varies by authority. It is advisable to complete mutation within 90 days of registration.

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

- **Buying without an EC:** Always get an Encumbrance Certificate for at least 30 years. An EC showing a mortgage that the seller claims is "discharged" but for which no formal release deed is registered is a red flag.
- **Underdeclaring the consideration:** Declaring a lower sale price to reduce stamp duty is illegal and exposes both buyer and seller to penalties. It also creates a cash component in the transaction — which has its own legal and financial risks.
- **Not verifying RERA registration for under-construction flats:** If you are buying a flat from a builder, check TS-RERA registration before booking. See our separate article on TS-RERA rights.
- **Power of Attorney sales:** Be extremely careful with transactions where the seller is acting through a Power of Attorney. Verify the PoA is properly registered, that the principal (the actual owner) is alive, and that the PoA has not been revoked. The Supreme Court has restricted title acquisition through unregistered PoA transactions.
- **Skipping legal advice:** Professional legal due diligence before a property purchase is not a luxury — it is risk management. The cost of a lawyer's opinion is a fraction of the cost of inheriting a disputed title.

Property registration in Hyderabad is a well-structured process when you know the steps. The investment in proper documentation, title verification, and legal guidance pays dividends for decades — because clean title is the foundation of everything that follows: resale, financing, inheritance, and development.
